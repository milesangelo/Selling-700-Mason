# Phase D: Pre-Inspection Awareness & Minor Cleanup

## Task Checklist

### Typical Inspection Callouts for Kennett Square, PA

This list reflects common issues found in home inspections for older homes in the Kennett Square area. Walk the property with this list and make "fix vs. disclose vs. price in" decisions for each item.

#### Exterior
- [ ] Grading and drainage around foundation (water pooling, negative slope)
- [ ] Gutters and downspouts (clogs, leaks, missing extensions)
- [ ] Siding condition (cracks, rot, loose sections)
- [ ] Stucco condition (already addressed in Phase B or quoted)
- [ ] Windows and doors (operation, seals, storms/screens)
- [ ] Roof condition (already addressed in Phase B or quoted)
- [ ] Chimney condition (cracks, missing cap, flashing)
- [ ] Walkways and steps (trip hazards, settling)
- [ ] Deck or porch (rot, railing strength, ledger board attachment)

#### Interior
- [ ] Windows (operation, broken seals/fogging, missing locks)
- [ ] Doors (operation, missing hardware, weather stripping)
- [ ] Floors (squeaks, soft spots, trip hazards)
- [ ] Walls and ceilings (cracks, water stains, plaster damage)
- [ ] Stairways (handrails secure, balusters tight, lighting)
- [ ] Bathroom ventilation (fans work, adequate ventilation)
- [ ] Kitchen GFCI outlets (already addressed in Phase B)
- [ ] Smoke and CO detectors (present, functional, up to code)
- [ ] Attic insulation and ventilation (adequate R-value, soffit vents clear)

#### Mechanical
- [ ] HVAC operation (already addressed in Phase B for mini-split)
- [ ] Furnace condition (age, service records, filter)
- [ ] Water heater condition (age, temperature setting, relief valve, drip pan)
- [ ] Plumbing leaks (under sinks, around toilets, in basement)
- [ ] Water pressure (adequate, not excessive >80 psi)
- [ ] Sump pump operation (if present, test with water)
- [ ] Electrical panel (already addressed in Phase B)
- [ ] Outlets and switches (already addressed in Phase B)
- [ ] GFCI and AFCI protection (up to current code requirements)

#### Basement/Crawlspace
- [ ] Foundation cracks (structural vs. cosmetic, moisture intrusion)
- [ ] Moisture or water damage (efflorescence, staining, mold)
- [ ] Sump pump and drainage
- [ ] Exposed wiring (already addressed in Phase B for junction boxes)
- [ ] Pest evidence (termites, carpenter ants, rodents)
- [ ] Insulation (if conditioned space)
- [ ] Radon (common in PA—consider pre-test or disclose)

### Self-Inspection Walkthrough
- [ ] Schedule 2-3 hours for thorough walkthrough
- [ ] Use the checklist above as a guide
- [ ] Take photos of any issues found
- [ ] Create a spreadsheet: Item | Location | Severity | Fix Cost | Decision (Fix/Disclose/Price In)
- [ ] Prioritize safety items (electrical, structural, moisture)
- [ ] Identify "easy wins" (minor fixes that boost confidence)

### Minor Repairs & Cosmetic Fixes
- [ ] Touch up paint in high-traffic areas
- [ ] Caulk around tubs, sinks, windows (fresh caulk reads as "maintained")
- [ ] Replace any burnt-out bulbs with matching color temperature
- [ ] Tighten loose doorknobs, cabinet pulls, towel bars
- [ ] Fix any sticky doors or windows
- [ ] Clean or replace HVAC filters
- [ ] Test and replace smoke detector batteries
- [ ] Oil squeaky hinges
- [ ] Replace cracked outlet or switch covers (if any missed in Phase B)
- [ ] Clean windows inside and out
- [ ] Pressure wash exterior (driveway, walkways, siding)
- [ ] Clear gutters and downspouts

### Disclosure Documentation
- [ ] Create a "Known Issues" document for items not repaired
- [ ] Include quotes for deferred repairs (roofing, stucco, etc.)
- [ ] Document all Phase A, B, C repairs with receipts and photos
- [ ] Prepare a "Maintenance and Repairs" binder for buyers
- [ ] Include manuals and warranties for appliances and systems
- [ ] Note any permit history or unpermitted work (transparency is key)

### Radon Considerations (PA-Specific)
- [ ] Research radon levels typical in Kennett Square area (PA is high-risk)
- [ ] Decide: pre-test and mitigate vs. let buyer test vs. disclose as unknown
- [ ] If testing, use certified radon professional
- [ ] If elevated, get mitigation quotes (typically $1,200-2,500)
- [ ] Consider mitigation as value-add vs. negotiation liability

### Final Staging Prep
- [ ] Deep clean entire house
- [ ] Remove personal items and clutter
- [ ] Neutralize decor (remove family photos, bold art, etc.)
- [ ] Maximize natural light (clean windows, open blinds for photos)
- [ ] Address any odors (pets, cooking, musty basement)
- [ ] Repair or remove any damaged furniture in photos
- [ ] Consider minor staging (flowers, fresh towels, bowl of fruit)

## Success Metrics
- Self-inspection completed with no surprises remaining
- All "fix" decisions completed and documented
- All "disclose" items documented with quotes or context
- Property is clean, neutral, and photo-ready
- Seller disclosure forms are complete and accurate

## Notes
- Transparency beats surprises—disclose known issues honestly
- Inspectors will find everything; better to address or disclose upfront
- Small fixes (caulk, paint touch-ups) have outsized impact on buyer perception
- Radon is common in PA—have a plan, don't ignore it
- Consider a pre-listing inspection to catch anything missed (optional but valuable)
- Clean and staged homes photograph better and show better, leading to stronger offers
